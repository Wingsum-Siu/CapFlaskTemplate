So What is Social Anxiety?

  • Social anxiety affects 19 million people in America and 1 out of 3 adolescents between the ages of 13 and 18. (1)

  • It is characterized to be a common anxiety disorder that can disrupt everyday behavior by blooming stress out of the fear of being humilated or viewed negatively. (5)

  • Psychological Symptoms (5)

    • Feeling an intense fear that one might humilate themselves, seem anxious to others, or be in a situation where one is viewed negatively by those around them

    • Avoiding speaking or engaging in situations where one might be the center of attention

    • Reflecting and overthinking on the negative flaws on one's performance

  • Physical Symptoms (5)

    • Trembling, blushing, and sweating

    • Upset stomach from nervousness

    • Muscle tension

    • Dizziness or blank mind

  • Performance type: one experienecs great anxiety speaking/performing in public but not in other situations

Past research suggests that adolescents are more sensitive to social evaluation.

  • Social experiment and journals indicate that adolescents are more likely to constantly feel watched where "they are under constant scrutiny by an imaginary audience." (2)

  • Adolescents have been recorded to release more cortisol, a stress hormone, when they feel watched. (3)

  • Negative social feedback cause teenagers more stress compared to adults. (3)

  • Compared to adults, teenagers spend more effort engaging their brains to understand and socialize with others, for the opinions of others remain much more influential and important to adolescents. (4)
